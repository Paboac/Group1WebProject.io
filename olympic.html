Olympic National Park

By: Kelly Chapman

Overview

Situated in a remote corner of northwest Washington state, Olympic National Park is known for its incredible scenery and varied landscapes. Encounters with the mythical Bigfoot is a very real possibility for those trekking through the park, which is defined by three ecosystems: subalpine forest and meadow, temperate forest, and an extra dramatic stretch of the Pacific coast.

Rainforest

Named after the Hoh river with its slate-hued waters running through, the Hoh Rain Forest is a dense and surreal canopy of moss covered trees. Both coniferous and deciduous species can be found here. Coastline

This 73 mile stretch is a rare, undeveloped piece of wilderness coast. In addition to the protected land, species living in the intertidal areas are also protected within the boundary of the Olympic Coast National Marine Sanctuary. While those living further offshore, a variety of seabirds, seals, and sealions also receive protected status within the Washington Maritime National Refuge Complex.
