Yellowstone National Park

By: Luiz Ramos

Yellowstone

Yellowstone was the first national park in the US, establish in 1876 by Ulysses Grant. It's the oldest continuously existing National Park in the world.

To this day, Yellowstone remains the second most visited national park in the United States to this day. It’s known for its geysers, of which there are over 500 of plus an additional 10,000 "thermal features", and its rich biodiversity. The park has the largest concentration of mammals in the continental United States, including grizzly bears, grey wolves, moose, buffalo and more.

Visitor activities

Although not every part of the 2.2 million acre park is accessible or trailable to the general public without a permit, there are more than 900 miles worth of hiking trails that are open during the spring and summer when the trails aren’t covered by snow and ice.

The park also contains several camp grounds and lodges where visitors can stay, but some are only open during the summer and spring. Additionally, the most famous geysers can be visited all years, except when the roads are closed due to dangerous weather.

Winter advisory

While the park is open during the winter, temperatures can reach as low as -22F (-30C), and many park services are not available. So be sure to prepare. Skiing and snowshoeing is possible on some trails during the winter, but beware of thermal pools covered in ice, and for avalanches.
